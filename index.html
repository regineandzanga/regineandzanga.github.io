About

Régine Salomé Andzanga is a sociolinguist, author, and educator from Cameroon with over two decades of university teaching and public service in language education. Her research examines the acquisition of French literacy in multilingual and bilingual contexts, with a focus on language policy, pedagogy, and cultural identity across Cameroon's dual education systems.

She holds a PhD in Linguistics from the Université Michel de Montaigne–Bordeaux 3 (2015), a Master's in Teaching French as a Second/Foreign Language from the University of Buea (2006), and a DIPES II from the École Normale Supérieure of the University of Yaoundé (1992). She taught at both the University of Buea (1996–2007) and the University of Yaoundé 1 (2016–2023), across the departments of French, Language Sciences, and the École Normale Supérieure.

She has served as Regional Pedagogical Inspector for Bilingualism, promoting French-language education to Anglophones in Cameroon's South-West and Centre regions, and held senior roles in the national examination system. She is a Chevalier des Palmes Académiques, a distinction awarded by the French government for outstanding contributions to education.

Alongside her scholarly work, she is a published novelist, playwright, and short-story writer. She is the founding president of the Association Camerounaise des Dames de la Plume, a women's literary association, and directs the Centre for the Preservation of African Languages.

Outside of work she grows mushrooms.

Research

Bilingual Education & Literacy

French reading and writing acquisition in Cameroon's Francophone and Anglophone school systems, drawing on fieldwork in the bilingual lycées of Yaoundé and Buea.

Sociolinguistics & Psycholinguistics

Code-switching, language contact, multilingual pedagogy, and the psychology of language acquisition in postcolonial contexts.

Language Policy & Education

Public education policy, cultural diversity, and the politics of language in Central Africa. FLE/FLS didactics and curriculum development for multilingual classrooms.

Language Preservation & African Identity

Documentation and revitalization of endangered African languages. Current research on Ewondo naming structures and how indigenous languages encode the foundations of communal living — toward a broader project of cultural reconciliation across the continent.
