About

Régine Salomé Andzanga is a sociolinguist and author from Cameroon. Her research examines the acquisition of French literacy in multilingual and bilingual educational contexts, with a focus on the interplay of language policy, pedagogy, and cultural identity across Cameroon's dual education systems.

She earned her PhD in Linguistics from the Université Michel de Montaigne–Bordeaux 3 in 2015, under the supervision of Professor Mwatha Musanji Ngalasso. Her doctoral work, based on extensive fieldwork in the bilingual lycées of Yaoundé and Buea, was published as a 458-page monograph by Éditions Proximité and is held at Stanford University Libraries.

Alongside her scholarly work, she is a published novelist and playwright. Her creative writing explores widowhood, gender, marriage, and the tensions between tradition and modernity in contemporary Cameroon.

She founded and directs the Centre for the Preservation of African Languages, dedicated to the documentation and revitalization of endangered languages in Central Africa.

Outside of work she grows mushrooms.

Research

French reading and writing acquisition in Cameroon's Francophone and Anglophone school systems, drawing on fieldwork in the bilingual lycées of Yaoundé and Buea.

Sociolinguistics

Code-switching, language contact, and multilingual pedagogy in postcolonial contexts.

Language Policy & Education

Public education policy, cultural diversity, and the politics of language in Central Africa.

Language Preservation

Documentation and revitalization of endangered African languages through community engagement and scholarly research.
